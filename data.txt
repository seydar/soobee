<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#cef2e0; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today.27s_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>

<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0;"><a href="/wiki/File:U2_3D_logo.svg" class="image" title="U2 3D logo"><img alt="U2 3D logo" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/U2_3D_logo.svg/100px-U2_3D_logo.svg.png" width="100" height="43" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/U2_3D_logo.svg/150px-U2_3D_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/U2_3D_logo.svg/200px-U2_3D_logo.svg.png 2x" /></a></div>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/U2_3D" title="U2 3D">U2 3D</a></b></i> is a 2008 <a href="/wiki/3D_film" title="3D film">3D</a> <a href="/wiki/Concert_film" title="Concert film">concert film</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a> performing during the <a href="/wiki/Vertigo_Tour" title="Vertigo Tour">Vertigo Tour</a> in 2006. The first live-action <a href="/wiki/Digital_3D" title="Digital 3D">digital 3D</a> film, it contains performances of 14&#160;songs, including tracks from <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Dismantle_an_Atomic_Bomb" title="How to Dismantle an Atomic Bomb">How to Dismantle an Atomic Bomb</a></i>, the album supported by the tour. The concert footage includes political and social statements made during the shows. The project was created to experiment with a new type of 3D film technology pioneered by producer Steve Schklair. After considering shooting <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> games in 3D, Schklair's company <a href="/wiki/3ality_Digital" title="3ality Digital" class="mw-redirect">3ality Digital</a> decided to create a concert film with U2. The band were hesitant to participate, but agreed to the project mainly as a technological experiment rather than a profit-making venture. Although set in Buenos Aires, <i>U2 3D</i> was shot at seven concerts across Latin America, and two in Australia. After a preview screening at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2007 Cannes Film Festival">2007 Cannes Film Festival</a>, <i>U2 3D</i> premiered at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2008 Sundance Film Festival">2008 Sundance Film Festival</a> and was released in late <span class="nowrap">January 2008</span>, exclusively in <a href="/wiki/IMAX_3D" title="IMAX 3D" class="mw-redirect">IMAX 3D</a> and digital 3D theaters. It peaked at number 19 at the United States box office, earned over $26&#160;million internationally (ranking as one of the highest-grossing concert films), and won several awards. (<a href="/wiki/U2_3D" title="U2 3D"><b>Full article...</b></a>)</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/File:J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg" class="image" title="William V, Prince of Orange"><img alt="William V, Prince of Orange" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg/84px-J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg" width="84" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg/126px-J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg/168px-J._G._Ziesenis_-_State_Portrait_of_Prince_William_V.jpg 2x" /></a></p>
</div>
<ul>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1795" title="1795">1795</a> – A day after <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Prince_of_Orange" title="William V, Prince of Orange">William V, Prince of Orange</a>, fled the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Revolution_in_Amsterdam" title="Batavian Revolution in Amsterdam">Batavian Revolution</a>, the <b><a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a></b> was established.</li>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1853" title="1853">1853</a> – <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Verdi" title="Giuseppe Verdi">Giuseppe Verdi</a>'s opera <i><b><a href="/wiki/Il_trovatore" title="Il trovatore">Il trovatore</a></b></i> was first performed at the <a href="/wiki/Tor_di_Nona" title="Tor di Nona">Teatro Apollo</a> in Rome.</li>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a> – A magnitude <span class="nowrap">6.8 <a href="/wiki/Surface_wave_magnitude" title="Surface wave magnitude">M<sub>s</sub></a></span> earthquake <b><a href="/wiki/1975_Kinnaur_earthquake" title="1975 Kinnaur earthquake">struck</a></b> northern <a href="/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh" title="Himachal Pradesh">Himachal Pradesh</a>, India, causing extensive damage to the region.</li>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1983" title="1983">1983</a> – The Nazi <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">SS</a> officer <b><a href="/wiki/Klaus_Barbie" title="Klaus Barbie">Klaus Barbie</a></b> was arrested in Bolivia, 32 years after the <a href="/wiki/Counterintelligence_Corps_(United_States_Army)" title="Counterintelligence Corps (United States Army)">US Army Counterintelligence Corps</a> helped him flee to Argentina.</li>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/2007" title="2007">2007</a> – <a href="/wiki/Armenians_in_Turkey" title="Armenians in Turkey">Turkish-Armenian</a> journalist <b><a href="/wiki/Hrant_Dink" title="Hrant Dink">Hrant Dink</a></b> was <b><a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Hrant_Dink" title="Assassination of Hrant Dink">assassinated</a></b> by a Turkish nationalist for his statements on the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>.</li>
</ul>
